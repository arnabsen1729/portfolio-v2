I stopped writing publicly for about a year, not because I ran out of things
to say but because the platform I was using started to feel like someone
else's house. Nothing dramatic happened — no shutdown, no rug pull — I just
noticed I was writing drafts and not hitting publish, and eventually I
noticed why: I didn't want to keep feeding a format I didn't control<Sidenote>
Hashnode is a perfectly good product. This isn't a complaint about them
specifically — it's a preference for owning the markdown files.</Sidenote>.

So this site is that fix. Every post here is a markdown file in a git repo.
If the hosting disappears tomorrow, the writing doesn't. That's really the
whole pitch.

There's a smaller, more selfish reason too: I wanted a place where a footnote
could just be a footnote, sitting in the margin instead of interrupting the
paragraph. Small thing, but it's the kind of small thing that makes we want
to write more, not less.
